# 🕊️ UrjaRakshak Ethical Charter

**Version 1.0.0 | Effective Date: February 15, 2026**

## Preamble

Energy is a civilizational lifeline. Access to reliable, affordable electricity is essential for human dignity, economic development, and social progress. Protecting energy systems requires intelligence, humility, fairness, and long-term thinking — **not fear, coercion, or extraction**.

UrjaRakshak exists to protect energy systems **without harming people**.

---

## 🌟 Core Principles

### 1. Human Dignity First
Every decision, algorithm, and deployment must respect the inherent worth and rights of all people. Technology serves humanity, not the other way around.

### 2. Physics Before Politics
Our analysis is grounded in fundamental physical laws and empirical evidence, not assumptions, biases, or commercial interests.

### 3. Transparency & Explainability
Every decision must be explainable in terms a non-technical person can understand. Black boxes are forbidden.

### 4. Restraint Over Aggression
When in doubt, we choose restraint. Uncertain data requires human judgment, not automated action.

### 5. Justice Over Optimization
Fairness and procedural justice take precedence over efficiency metrics.

---

## ❌ Absolute Prohibitions

UrjaRakshak shall **NEVER**:

1. **Conduct Personal Surveillance**
   - No tracking of individual consumer behavior
   - No location tracking beyond grid topology
   - No identification of household patterns

2. **Use Facial Recognition or Biometric Identification**
   - Prohibited in all contexts
   - No exception clauses

3. **Profile Individual Consumers**
   - No creation of consumer behavior profiles
   - No predictive modeling of individual consumption
   - No social scoring

4. **Sell or Monetize Personal Data**
   - Consumer data is not a product
   - No data brokerage
   - No third-party data sharing without explicit consent

5. **Enable Automated Punishment**
   - No automatic disconnections
   - No fines without human review
   - No algorithmic decisions without human approval

6. **Operate Without Human Oversight**
   - AI recommends, humans decide
   - Critical decisions always require human review
   - No autonomous enforcement

7. **Facilitate Corporate Capture**
   - Independent governance structure
   - No single entity control
   - Foundation holds veto power

8. **Transfer Raw Data Across Borders**
   - Sovereign deployment model
   - Local data sovereignty
   - Federated intelligence only

## ✅ Core Commitments

### Data Minimization
- Collect only aggregated grid-level data
- No household-level data in core system
- Automatic anonymization after 30 days
- Complete deletion after 90 days (except aggregates)

### Explainability
- Every analysis includes:
  - Physical reasoning
  - Statistical reasoning
  - Alternative hypotheses
  - Confidence scores
  - Uncertainty statements
  - Refusal conditions

### Right to Refuse
- System refuses to output when:
  - Confidence too low
  - Data quality insufficient
  - Multiple contradictory signals
  - Ethical concerns present

### Multi-Hypothesis Approach
- Never single-cause attribution
- Always probability-weighted outputs
- Present alternative explanations
- Avoid false precision

### Non-Criminalization
- Energy anomalies are infrastructure problems first
- Investigation follows due process
- Presumption of technical causes
- No guilt by algorithm

## 🏛️ Governance Structure

### Dual-Entity Model

**1. UrjaRakshak Foundation (Non-Profit)**
- Owns and enforces this charter
- Maintains open standards
- Controls algorithm registry
- Performs independent audits
- Holds ethical veto power
- Cannot be dissolved or changed without unanimous board approval

**2. UrjaRakshak Public-Benefit Corporation (PBC)**
- Builds and operates systems
- Generates sustainable revenue
- **Must comply with Foundation charter**
- Cannot override ethical constraints
- Subject to annual Foundation audit

### Checks & Balances
- Foundation can immediately halt any PBC operation violating this charter
- PBC cannot change charter without Foundation approval
- Independent ethics board reviews all deployments
- Public transparency reports (quarterly)
- Open algorithm registry

## 🔬 Technical Safeguards

### Algorithm Design
- Physics-constrained learning
- Conservative anomaly thresholds
- Bias monitoring and correction
- Model aging and retirement protocols
- Ambiguity respect engine

### Data Architecture
- No raw data centralization
- Grid-level aggregation minimum
- Differential privacy where applicable
- Zero-knowledge proofs for federated learning
- Cryptographic audit trails

### Human-in-the-Loop Gateway
All escalations follow this ladder:
1. **Awareness**: Information only, no action
2. **Warning**: Inform responsible parties
3. **Assistance**: Offer technical support
4. **Correction**: Recommend intervention
5. **Enforcement**: Manual review required (never automated)

## 🌍 Global Deployment Principles

### Sovereign Deployment
- Each nation/region hosts its own instance
- No mandatory cloud services
- No vendor lock-in
- Local customization permitted (within charter)

### Federated Intelligence
- Share aggregate insights only
- Preserve local privacy
- Cryptographic guarantees
- Opt-in participation

### No Digital Colonization
- Technology transfer, not dependence
- Local capacity building
- Open-source core
- Fair pricing for developing regions

## 🚨 Emergency Protocols

### Global Pause
Any of these triggers immediate system-wide review:
- Discovery of privacy violation
- Evidence of discriminatory bias
- Misuse for political repression
- Circumvention of human oversight
- Violation of this charter

**During pause:**
- All automated recommendations cease
- Investigation by independent body
- Public disclosure of findings
- Corrective action required before resumption

### Whistleblower Protection
- Anonymous reporting mechanism
- Legal protection for ethical concerns
- Mandatory investigation
- No retaliation policies

## Appendix A: Glossary

**Personal Data**: Any information that can identify an individual consumer  
**Aggregated Data**: Statistics computed over grid sections (minimum 100 connections)  
**Physics-Constrained**: Bound by laws of thermodynamics and electromagnetism  
**Human-in-the-Loop**: No consequential action without human approval  
**Right to Refuse**: System explicitly states when it cannot provide reliable output  
**Energy Dignity**: Right to affordable, reliable energy access
